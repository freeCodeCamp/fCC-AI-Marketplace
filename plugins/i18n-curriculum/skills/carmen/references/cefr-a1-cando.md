Source: Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR)

## Overall A1 Profile

> Can understand and use familiar everyday expressions and very basic phrases aimed
> at the satisfaction of needs of a concrete type. Can introduce themselves and
> others and can ask and answer questions about personal details such as where they
> live, people they know and things they have. Can interact in a simple way provided
> the other person talks slowly and clearly and is prepared to help.

---

## Listening (A1)

- **A1.L.1** — Can recognize familiar words and very basic phrases concerning
  themselves, their family and immediate concrete surroundings when people speak
  slowly and clearly.
- **A1.L.2** — Can understand simple questions and instructions addressed carefully
  and slowly.
- **A1.L.3** — Can follow very short, simple conversations on familiar topics
  (greetings, introductions) when delivered slowly.

## Reading (A1)

- **A1.R.1** — Can recognize familiar names, words, and very simple sentences on
  notices, posters, or in catalogues.
- **A1.R.2** — Can understand very short, simple texts, picking out familiar names
  and words.
- **A1.R.3** — Can read and understand simple personal forms (name, nationality,
  address, age, profession).

## Speaking — Spoken Interaction (A1)

- **A1.SI.1** — Can interact in a simple way, but communication is totally dependent
  on repetition, rephrasing and repair.
- **A1.SI.2** — Can ask and answer simple questions, initiate and respond to simple
  statements in areas of immediate need or on very familiar topics.
- **A1.SI.3** — Can make simple purchases where pointing or other gesture can support
  the interaction.
- **A1.SI.4** — Can handle very short social exchanges using everyday polite forms
  of greeting and address.

## Speaking — Spoken Production (A1)

- **A1.SP.1** — Can produce simple mainly isolated phrases about people and places.
- **A1.SP.2** — Can describe their place of residence and the people they know using
  simple language.
- **A1.SP.3** — Can introduce themselves (name, profession, nationality, age).

## Writing (A1)

- **A1.W.1** — Can write a short, simple postcard.
- **A1.W.2** — Can fill in forms with personal details (name, address, nationality).
- **A1.W.3** — Can write simple isolated phrases and sentences.

## Framework → CEFR Mapping Notes

When selecting concepts for **A1-only** modules, apply these filters regardless
of the official framework (PCIC, QCER, CECRL, etc.):

- ✅ Include: Concepts that enable the communicative functions listed above
- ✅ Include: High-frequency, concrete, situational language
- ⚠️ Flag for A2: Complex tenses beyond the present, reflexive verbs (advanced
  use), compound sentences with subordination
- ❌ Exclude: Low-frequency grammar, abstract notions, professional register
  beyond basic job titles
